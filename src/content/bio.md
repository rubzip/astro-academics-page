
I am a theoretical physicist whose work during the *Annus Mirabilis* of 1905 has fundamentally reshaped our understanding of the universe. My career has been dedicated to bridging the gaps between classical mechanics and the then-emerging fields of quantum theory and relativity.

## My Life

Born in Ulm, Germany, my journey began not with a grand laboratory, but with a simple compass and a deep curiosity about the invisible forces that govern the physical world. After my studies at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich, I spent several formative years at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern. It was in the quiet intervals of evaluating patent applications that I formulated the theories that would eventually challenge the Newtonian worldview. I believe that imagination is more important than knowledge, for knowledge is limited, while imagination embraces the entire world.

## Current Work

Currently, my focus is divided between the refinement of General Relativity and the search for a Unified Field Theory—a single mathematical framework that can encompass both gravity and electromagnetism. I am also deeply engaged in pedagogical efforts at the Institute for Advanced Study, where I strive to make complex physical concepts accessible to the next generation of thinkers. In my spare time, I often find clarity in music, particularly playing the violin, which I consider as essential to my life as my equations.

## Research Interests

My research focus includes: Electrodynamics, Thermodynamics, Quantum Mechanics, and General Relativity.
